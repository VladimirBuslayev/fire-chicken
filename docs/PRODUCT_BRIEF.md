# Illustrated — Product Brief

## Product vision

Illustrated is a premium visual archive and collection companion for Pokémon card collectors.

It helps collectors browse, understand, organize, and gradually build their physical card collection around the artists, sets, cards, and artwork they care about.

## Core positioning

Illustrated should not feel like a generic tracker.

It should feel like:

- a curated card archive
- a personal collector vault
- a visual gallery
- a binder companion
- an artist-focused discovery tool

## Primary user

A physical Pokémon card collector who:

- buys cards online
- trades with friends
- attends card shows
- cares about artwork and illustrators
- wants a better collection experience than a spreadsheet
- may still want pricing references, but does not want prices treated as perfect truth

## Differentiation

Most collection apps are organized around:

- card name
- set
- market value
- owned/not owned

Illustrated should be organized around:

- illustrators
- artwork
- visual discovery
- collection progress
- sets and variants
- collector context

Artist-first browsing is a strategic strength.

## Product pillars

### 1. Visual archive

Beautiful browsing by artist, set, era, language, and artwork.

### 2. Personal collection

Owned/missing states, CSV import, manual corrections, wishlist, binder/share views.

### 3. Collector intelligence

Pricing references, eBay sold links, value confidence, last-updated context.

### 4. Curation layer

Manual corrections, artist aliases, notable cards, missing API data, editorial quality.

## Near-term product goal

Make the current MVP stable enough to support a modular migration and future product expansion.

## Long-term product goal

Become a polished collector app that feels more premium, visual, and emotionally satisfying than generic card trackers.

## Product promise

Build a beautiful visual archive of your Pokémon card collection — organized by the artists, sets, and cards you care about.
